How to Read This Indicator

Higher recession probability readings generally reflect a flatter or more inverted Treasury yield curve. A positive and more normally sloped curve tends to correspond with lower recession risk readings.

Methodology

The Recession Probability Monitor is a BondStats macro indicator based on the shape of the US Treasury yield curve. It uses the 10Y-3M and 10Y-2Y spreads as recession-related market signals.

The model estimates recession risk by assigning greater weight to deeper yield curve inversions, especially in the 10Y-3M spread, which has historically been one of the most closely watched recession indicators.

  • Indicator focus: US Treasury yield curve structure
  • Main inputs: 10Y-3M spread and 10Y-2Y spread
  • Output: Estimated recession probability over the next 12 months
  • Use case: Macro monitoring and educational analysis

This is a BondStats model for informational and educational purposes only. It is not an official forecast and should not be interpreted as investment advice.
